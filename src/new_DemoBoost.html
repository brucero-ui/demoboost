How to use DemoBoost

★ Recommended demo workflow

  • What this tool is for: DemoBoost pre-caches your Dynamics 365 sites in the background so they load instantly during your live demo — no cold-start delays in front of customers.
  • Run DemoBoost before every demo so each site is loaded, warmed, and cached in this tab's memory while you finish prepping.
  • Click ▶ Start and let the rotator make at least one full pass through your sites — this hydrates the in-memory pool and gives you fast switches.
  • After a full rotation completes, the demo Launch buttons (Launch CSW Demo, Launch CE Demo, Launch FS Demo) become visible in the right-side panel. Click the one matching your demo — or click Open Apps Page — to navigate this same tab to your environment's apps page, keeping all cached content alive in memory.
  • Stay in this tab for the demo. Opening a new tab discards the in-memory cache and forces a cold reload.

★ Not sure where to start?

  • If your Sites list is empty, click Load suggested Dynamics sites in the empty Sites panel to get a curated starter set — a great way to see how the tool works without typing any URLs.
  • Pick an app from the Seed for app dropdown (Sales Hub, Copilot Service workspace, Field Service, Customer Insights – Journeys, or All apps), then enter your Dynamics 365 org domain when prompted.
  • The booster seeds a mix of list and record views so the rotator has realistic content to cycle through immediately.
  • Once seeded, follow the Recommended demo workflow above: click ▶ Start and let the rotator make at least one full pass to warm the cache, then click a Launch button (CSW / CE / FS) — or Open Apps Page — when you're ready to present.

1 · Add Sites

  • Paste a URL into the Add New Site field in the Sites panel on the left.
  • Optionally enter a Site Name to give it a friendly label.
  • Click ➕ Add Site (or press Enter) to save it to the list.
  • Use the Edit button on any item to change its URL or name, then click Update.
  • Use the Delete button to remove a single site, or 🗑️ Clear All to start fresh.
  • Drag the ⋮⋮ handle on any item to reorder the rotation sequence.

2 · Start the Rotator

  • Set the ⏱️ Interval (in seconds) — this controls how long each site is displayed before automatically advancing to the next one.
  • Click Apply to save the interval, then click ▶ Start to begin rotating.
  • The countdown in the header shows how many seconds remain before the next rotation.
  • The currently displayed site is highlighted in the Sites list.
  • Click ⏹ Stop at any time to pause rotation.

3 · Manual Navigation

  • While the rotator is running you can still click any site's Go button to jump to it immediately.
  • The rotator will continue from the site you jumped to on the next tick.

4 · Export & Import

  • Give your session a label using the Demo Name field.
  • Click 📥 Export to download a .json file containing your sites, name, and interval setting.
  • Click 📤 Import to load a previously exported file and restore your full configuration.

5 · Analytics Panel

  • The 📊 Analytics panel on the right tracks: total rotations, session time, and per-page view counts.
  • Load Event — time until the page's network resources finish loading.
  • Render Done — time until all DOM activity and animations fully settle (a more complete measure of "ready").
  • vs Baseline — compares the most recent render time against the slowest seen this session. A positive percentage means the page loaded faster than its worst recorded time.
  • Click 🔄 Reset Analytics to clear all collected metrics.

6 · Collapsing Panels

  • Click the thin vertical tab strip on the inner edge of either panel to collapse or expand it, giving more space to the live preview.

7 · Persistent site cache

  • Once a site has been loaded, it stays alive in the background for up to 3 hours. Rotating back to it is instant — no reload.
  • Switching to a cached site preserves its scroll position and any in-page state (open dialogs, applied filters, etc.).
  • The browser's HTTP cache is automatically shared across all tabs in the same browser profile. Each site is prefetched on add to nudge the browser to retain its responses; the remote server's caching headers still apply.
  • Note: Authenticated apps (including Dynamics 365) typically send Cache-Control: no-store, which means their responses can't be reused across tabs. Within this tab, however, the in-memory pool keeps them live so switching is instant.

8 · Tips & Notes

  • Use the Share link from any Dynamics List or Record view for best results — these URLs load with full performance optimisations applied.
    Look for this button in the Dynamics command bar:
    Share ⌄
  • It is not necessary to use the hero record for a given demo — any similar record will achieve the same performance boost.
  • Some sites block iframe embedding via security headers; if a page appears blank, try the direct share URL instead.
  • Your sites and settings are saved automatically in your browser's local storage — they persist between sessions on the same device.
  • This tool is intended for demonstrations only and is not supported in production environments.

👋 Welcome to DemoBoost

DemoBoost pre-caches your Dynamics 365 sites in the background so they load instantly during your live demo — no cold-start delays in front of customers.

  1. Not sure where to start? If your Sites list is empty, click Load suggested Dynamics sites, pick the app you're demoing from Seed for app, and enter your org domain. A curated set of list and record views will be added for you.
  2. Let it warm up. Click ▶ Start and let the rotator make at least one full pass through your sites. Each page is loaded and held in memory.
  3. Launch your demo from this same tab. Once a full rotation completes, the Launch CSW Demo / Launch CE Demo / Launch FS Demo buttons appear in the right-hand panel. Click the one matching your demo — or click Open Apps Page — to start. The cache stays alive only while this tab does, so don't open a new one.
